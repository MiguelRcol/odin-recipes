Mondongo Recipe

Mondongo is a hearty Latin American stew made with beef tripe, pork, chorizo, and vegetables, simmered with spices until rich and flavorful.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of beef tripe
  • 750 grams of lean pork
  • 3 Colombian or cooking chorizos
  • 1 tomato
  • 2 stalks of spring onion (green onion)
  • 1 small onion
  • 4 potatoes
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste
  • ½ teaspoon of ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon of annatto (achiote)
  • A handful of fresh cilantro
  • Juice of one lemon
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Clean the beef tripe thoroughly under cold running water and cut it into small pieces.
  2. Place the tripe in a large pot, cover with water, and boil for about 1 hour or until tender. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a separate pot, add the lean pork and chorizos. Cover with water and cook for 30–40 minutes.
  4. Add the cooked tripe to the pot with the pork and chorizos.
  5. Chop the tomato, onions, garlic, and cilantro. Add them to the pot.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, ground cumin, and annatto (achiote).
  7. Add the peeled and diced potatoes.
  8. Pour in enough water to cover all ingredients and simmer over medium heat for about 45 minutes, until the stew thickens and the potatoes are tender.
  9. Add the lemon juice and adjust seasoning to taste.
  10. Serve hot, garnished with a bit of fresh cilantro on top.
